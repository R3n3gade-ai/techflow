SECTION 1 — GOVERNING PRINCIPLE 
Prime Directive 
The PM needs to be at the lunch. The system runs the portfolio. Every eliminatable human touchpoint  is evidence of an incomplete build. 
This document specifies the complete automated deployment and execution architecture for ARMS v4.0. PM  investment decisions — made once at the strategic level — are translated into autonomous, rules-based machine  execution at every subsequent step. The human role is intelligence and governance. The system role is  everything else. 
1.1 What Automation Covers 
• Regime detection and scoring — continuous, no human input required. 
• Portfolio rebalancing — triggered by regime transitions, CDF decay, or PDS thresholds. • Order construction and submission — LAEP-tiered, broker API executed. 
• SLOF sizing and adjustment — regime-gated, automated by Decision Engine. 
• PTRH sizing and roll — CAM autonomous every 5 minutes. Never in PM queue. 
1.2 What Stays With the PM 
• Gate 6 / Category A thesis initiation — new position ideas entering the queue. 
• Regime override declarations — manual CRASH escalation in extreme events. 
• Whitelist approval of new securities before the system can trade them. 
• Annual architecture review and version approval.

SECTION 3 — REGIME ENGINE & TRIGGER LOGIC 
The regime engine runs every 5 minutes during market hours and produces a single composite score (0.00–1.00).  That score determines the active regime, which sets the equity allocation ceiling and governs SLOF and PTRH  behavior. 
3.1 Regime Score Bands 
Regime  | Score   Range | Equity   Ceiling | SLOF Status  | PTRH Status
RISK_ON  | 0.00 – 0.35  | 100%  | Active — full size  | Active — baseline 1.2% NAV
WATCH  | 0.36 – 0.50  | 100%  | Active — full size  | Active — CAM sized
NEUTRAL  | 0.51 – 0.65  | 75%  | Reduced  | Active — CAM sized, elevated
DEFENSIVE  | 0.66 – 0.80  | 40%  | Removed entirely  | Active — CAM sized, maximum
CRASH  | 0.81 – 1.00  | 15%  | Removed entirely  | Active — CAM sized, maximum



3.2 Composite Score Inputs 
Sub-Score  | Inputs & Weight
Credit Stress Index  | HYG/LQD spread, IG CDS, TED spread — 25% weight
Volatility Regime  | VIX level, VIX/VIX3M ratio, realized vol — 25% weight
Macro Momentum  | PMI trend, yield curve shape, rate hike probability — 20% weight
Liquidity Conditions  | Fed balance sheet delta, repo rates, dollar index — 20% weight
Equity Internals  | Breadth, momentum dispersion, sector rotation — 10% weight



3.3 Regime Transition Confirmation Rules 
• Upgrade (e.g. DEFENSIVE → NEUTRAL): score must hold below upper boundary for 3 consecutive 5- min cycles (15 min). 
• Downgrade (e.g. NEUTRAL → DEFENSIVE): score must breach boundary for 2 consecutive cycles (10  min). Asymmetric by design — defense is faster than offense. 
• CRASH exception: any single cycle crossing 0.81 triggers CRASH immediately. No confirmation delay.  Survival before precision.

SECTION 5 — PTRH: PORTFOLIO TAIL RISK HEDGE 
Canonical Definition 
PTRH is the portfolio's permanent defensive hedge. One instrument only: QQQ outright puts. Always  on. CAM sizes it autonomously every 5 minutes. PTRH never appears in the PM decision queue. The  PM is informed of sizing — never asked to approve it. 
5.1 PTRH Instrument Rules — Absolute 
• Instrument: QQQ outright puts only. No single-stock puts. No put spreads. No exceptions. • Expiry: 90–120 day target at purchase. Roll at 30 DTE automatically. 
• Baseline floor: 1.2% NAV minimum. CAM cannot size below this under any regime. 
• VIX entry preference: CAM prefers sub-22 VIX for new premium deployment. Above 28, CAM holds  existing positions and does not add new puts. 
• Roll mechanic: spread order — buy new expiry, sell expiring leg simultaneously via LAEP. 
5.2 CAM — Autonomous PTRH Sizer 
CAM (src/engine/cam.py) runs every 5 minutes immediately after the regime engine produces its composite  score. It outputs a single number: target PTRH notional as a percentage of NAV. If actual PTRH notional deviates  from target by more than 5%, CAM auto-corrects at the next available execution window. 
5.3 CAM Four Multipliers 
Multiplier  | Logic
Regime Score Multiplier  | Linear scale: score 0.50 = 1.0x base. Score 0.80 = 2.4x. Score 1.00 = 3.0x.  Higher regime stress = larger hedge notional.
Equity Exposure Multiplier  | Scales proportionally with current equity book as % of NAV. More equity  deployed = more hedge required.
FEM Concentration Multiplier  | Activates above 65% factor concentration (WATCH). ALERT (>80%) adds  40% to hedge notional.
Macro Stress Multiplier  | Activates on SSL scenario detection (S1–S8). Ranges 1.0x–2.0x  depending on scenario severity.

SECTION 6 — SLOF: SYNTHETIC LONG OFFENSIVE FRAMEWORK 
Canonical Definition 
SLOF is the portfolio's offensive leverage instrument. It is not a hedge. It amplifies upside exposure in  favorable regimes using an ATM synthetic long: buy call + sell put, same strike, same expiry,  combined delta +1.0, net debit. SLOF is regime-gated and automated by the Decision Engine. 
6.1 SLOF Structure 
• Legs: Buy ATM call + Sell ATM put. Same underlying, same strike, same expiry. • Net position delta: +1.0 (equivalent to long 100 shares per contract pair). 
• Cost: net debit (call premium exceeds put premium at ATM). 
• Purpose: amplify equity upside without adding cash to the long book. Offensive, not defensive. 6.2 SLOF Regime Gates — Automated 
Regime  | SLOF Status
RISK_ON  | Active — full size. Decision Engine deploys SLOF at maximum authorized  notional.
WATCH  | Active — full size. No reduction. Upside environment still favorable.
NEUTRAL  | Reduced. Decision Engine scales SLOF notional proportionally with equity  ceiling reduction (75%).
DEFENSIVE  | Removed entirely. Decision Engine unwinds all SLOF legs at regime  transition. No exceptions.
CRASH  | Removed entirely. SLOF is incompatible with crisis posture. Only PTRH  remains active.



6.3 SLOF Automation Rules 
• SLOF sizing and adjustment are fully automated by the Decision Engine on every regime transition. 
• No PM approval required for SLOF adjustments. PM receives EOD confirmation of SLOF status and  notional. 
• SLOF legs are always executed as a spread order — both legs simultaneously. No legged risk. 
• At DEFENSIVE trigger: Decision Engine unwinds SLOF before deploying any equity trims. Sequence  matters.

LAEP (Liquidity-Adjusted Execution Protocol) converts a target allocation into actual broker orders. It accounts for  VIX regime, position size relative to ADV, and spread conditions before submitting to the broker API. 
7.1 VIX Execution Tiers 
Tier  | VIX Range  | Condition  | Execution Behavior
1  | < 16  | Normal  | Limit orders at mid. Aggressive fill targeting.
2  | 16 – 22  | Elevated  | Limit orders 0.1–0.2% through mid. Patience mode.
3  | 22 – 28  | High  | VWAP execution for large sizes. Spread-aware limits.
4  | > 28  | Stress  | TWAP over full session. Single-order size reduced 50%.
5  | > 40  | Crisis  | Execution halted except CRASH protocol. PM alert.



7.2 Position Size Constraints 
• Maximum single-order size: 20% of 10-day ADV for that security. 
• Positions exceeding 20% ADV: LAEP splits into multiple child orders across the session. • Minimum fill: 80% of target allocation within session. Remainder carries as resting limit. • Slippage budget: 0.15% maximum vs. decision-time price. Exceeded = order cancelled, PM alert. 
7.3 Order Types by Scenario
Scenario  | Order Behavior
Standard deployment (VIX <  22) | Limit at mid-market. 30-min fill window before stepping to offer.
High-volatility deployment  | VWAP algo. 15% volume participation. Full session.
CRASH exit (forced de-risk)  | Market orders for top 5 positions by ADV. Limits for illiquid.
SLOF leg execution  | Spread order: both legs simultaneously. No legged risk ever.
PTRH roll (CAM-triggered)  | Spread order: buy new expiry + sell expiring leg simultaneously.
CDF-triggered trim  | Limit sell at bid+1. If unfilled in 60 min, steps to bid.
PDS auto-ceiling (-12% or - 18%) | Immediate market sell to target ceiling. No fill-price optimization.

SECTION 8 — AUTOMATED RISK MODULES 
8.1 PDS — Portfolio Drawdown Sentinel 
Drawdown from Peak NAV  | System Response
-8%  | WARNING. PM alert pushed immediately. No automatic action. PM  reviews.
-12%  | AUTO: equity ceiling hard-capped at 60% of NAV. LAEP executes trims  immediately.
-18%  | AUTO: equity ceiling hard-capped at 30% of NAV. Full de-risk sequence  initiates.



8.2 CDF — Conviction Decay Function 
• 45 days without PM thesis update: position auto-trimmed to 80% of target allocation. • 90 days without PM thesis update: position auto-trimmed to 60% of target allocation. • PM resets the CDF clock by submitting a thesis confirmation via dashboard. One action, one click. • New positions (GOOGL, GEV, CEG, VST, BE) — CDF clock starts at deployment date. 
8.3 FEM — Factor Exposure Monitor 
• Runs at every regime cycle AND as a pre-flight check before any new deployment order. • WATCH threshold: >65% factor concentration in any single theme. Alert to PM. Orders proceed. 
• ALERT threshold: >80% factor concentration. New orders in that theme are blocked until concentration  normalizes. 
8.4 SSL — Stress Scenario Library 
• 8 pre-defined macro stress scenarios. Each carries a signature pattern in the composite sub-inputs. • Pattern match >70% confidence: SSL flags the scenario and adjusts CAM macro stress multiplier. • Active Scenarios: S1: Rate Shock. S2: Credit Event. S3: Geopolitical Escalation. S4: Liquidity Freeze. • S5: Dollar Spike. S6: Energy Price Shock. S7: Tech Earnings Collapse. S8: Systemic Contagion.

SECTION 11 — SECURITY, COMPLIANCE & FAILURE PROTOCOLS 
11.1 API Key & Credential Management 
• All API keys stored in environment variables only. Never in code or version control. • Alpaca and IBKR credentials rotated quarterly minimum. 
• Read-only key for market data. Separate trading-enabled key for execution. Principle of least privilege. • All outbound API calls logged: timestamp, endpoint, payload hash, response status. 
11.2 Kill Switch & Order Validation 
• LAEP pre-submission validation: ticker on whitelist, allocation within regime ceiling, FEM pre-check, ADV  constraint, slippage budget. 
• Any order failing validation is rejected, logged, and PM alerted. No exceptions. 
• Hard kill switch: PM halts all new order submission via dashboard toggle. Open orders cancelled.  Positions held. 
11.3 System Failure Handling 
Failure Mode  | System Response
Regime engine offline  | Last known regime score held. No new orders until restored. PM alert  immediately.
Broker API failure  | Failover to IBKR within 30 seconds. Both fail: all pending orders cancelled,  positions held.
Data feed interruption  | ARMS holds last known prices. Regime score pauses. CAM holds current  PTRH sizing.
CAM module crash  | PTRH held at current notional. PM notified. Manual review required before  next session.
Dashboard offline  | EOD snapshot emailed directly to PM. System continues operating  normally.
