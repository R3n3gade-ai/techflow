SECTION 1 — THE ARC 
From Automated Execution to Autonomous Intelligence 
ARMS is not a trading system. It is an intelligence-execution architecture — a machine that translates a PM's pre consensus thesis work into precise, disciplined portfolio action at speeds and consistency no human can match.  The arc of its evolution follows a single logic: the system earns autonomy by demonstrating accuracy. It is not  granted authority. It proves it. 
The PM builds the rules. The system earns the right to extend them. 
Version 1 automates execution of pre-defined rules. Version 2 automates intelligence capture and performance  attribution. Full autonomy grants the system the right to initiate positions when its signal accuracy has been  validated across a full market cycle. Each version is a gate, not a timeline. The system advances when it earns it  — not on a calendar.

SECTION 3 — VERSION 2: INTELLIGENCE ARCHITECTURE 
After Version 1 Is Live and Validated 
Version 2 is not scheduled. It begins when Version 1 has demonstrated stable live operation across at least one  full regime cycle — a complete transition from RISK_ON through DEFENSIVE and back. That is the gate. Not a  date. 
3.1 Thesis Repository 
A structured per-position database that captures the original Gate 1–6 thesis, invalidation conditions, and every  PM update over the life of the position. The system reads it. CDF checks it. CAM references it. The PM's  intelligence moves from his head and chat logs into institutional memory that persists regardless of who is in the  room. 
Component  | Description
Thesis Record  | Per-position structured entry: original thesis, conviction tier, Gate score,  invalidation conditions, entry date, PM notes.
Invalidation Conditions  | PM-defined conditions under which a position is flagged for Priority 1 review  regardless of CDF clock or regime state.
CDF Integration  | CDF checks thesis update timestamp — not just a dashboard confirmation  click. Richer signal of actual PM engagement.
Version History  | Every thesis update is versioned. The system can show how the PM's view  on a position evolved over time.
Query Interface  | PM can query the repository: 'Show all positions where invalidation condition  involves memory bandwidth.' Intelligence becomes searchable.



3.2 EDR Compressed Latency Architecture 
The Early Detection Radar graduates from a dashboard alert to an active pipeline participant. When a CRITICAL  signal fires, the system does not wait for the PM to notice. It acts — up to the boundary of its authority. 
Signal Level  | System Response in Version 2
LOW  | Logged. Appears in EOD snapshot. No PM alert.
MEDIUM  | Dashboard flag. PM reviews at next session. No queue action.
HIGH  | PM alert pushed immediately. System prepares draft thesis and preliminary sizing. PM  reviews pre-populated entry — not a blank page.
CRITICAL  | PM alert pushed immediately with 4-hour response window. System prepares full draft  queue entry with thesis, sizing, entry range, and order type pre-populated from signal  data. PM approves, modifies, or rejects. If no response in 4 hours, system defaults to  PM-pre-specified behavior per signal type.

SECTION 4 — FULL AUTONOMY: VERSION 3 
Earned After a Full Market Cycle 
Full autonomy is not a feature. It is a credential. The system earns it by demonstrating statistically significant  signal accuracy across a complete market cycle — at minimum one full transition from RISK_ON through CRASH  and back to RISK_ON. No calendar date grants this. Only track record does. 
The system does not get autonomy because it is capable. It gets autonomy  because it has proven it. 
4.1 Autonomy Gate Requirements 
• Full live operation across at least one complete regime cycle (RISK_ON → WATCH → NEUTRAL →  DEFENSIVE → CRASH → recovery). 
• EDR CRITICAL signal hit rate above 65% on 20 or more signals — meaning 65% of CRITICAL signals  that were acted upon produced positive thesis outcomes. 
• Zero critical system failures across the observation period — no unhandled exceptions, no missed regime  transitions, no order routing failures. 
• PM formal sign-off on autonomy grant per signal type. Autonomy is granted signal-by-signal, not system wide. Dark pool accumulation may earn autonomy before earnings language inflection does. 
• Legal and compliance review of autonomous position initiation authority in the context of fund structure  and broker agreements. 
4.2 What Full Autonomy Looks Like 
In Version 3, the EDR is granted the authority to initiate positions within strictly defined parameters — without PM  confirmation — on signal types that have cleared the autonomy gate:
Parameter  | Constraint
Maximum autonomous position  size | 1.0% of NAV per initiation. PM can adjust per signal type after track  record review.
Signal type restriction  | Only signal types that have individually cleared the 65% hit rate gate.  Not all EDR signals simultaneously.
Regime restriction  | Autonomous initiation only in RISK_ON and WATCH regimes.  NEUTRAL requires PM confirmation. DEFENSIVE and CRASH: no  initiation.
Whitelist restriction  | Autonomous initiation only in securities already on PM whitelist. New  securities always require PM approval.
PM notification  | PM receives immediate notification of every autonomous initiation. Can  reverse within 30 minutes at no penalty. After 30 minutes, position is  live.

4.3 What Never Becomes Autonomous 
• Thesis invalidation decisions — the PM always makes the final call on closing a thesis position. • Regime override declarations — only the PM can escalate to CRASH manually. • New security whitelist additions — PM gatekeeps what the system is permitted to trade. • Architecture parameter changes — version updates always require PM approval. • Positions above 2.0% of NAV — meaningful conviction positions always originate from PM thesis work.
