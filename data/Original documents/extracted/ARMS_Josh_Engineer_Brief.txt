THE PRIMARY DIRECTIVE 
ARMS automates the translation of regime state into portfolio action — so that when the market moves, the portfolio responds correctly, 
immediately, and without the PM being in the room. 
WHAT THIS MEANS IN PLAIN LANGUAGE 
The PM makes one decision: which companies to own and why. 
Everything after that decision is your system's job. 
When the macro environment shifts — a war, a rate shock, a credit event — ARMS detects it, scores it, classifies it,  and executes every consequence of that classification. Automatically. Completely. Without a phone call.

THREE RULES THAT GOVERN EVERYTHING YOU BUILD 
Rule 1 — The PM queue must shrink over time, never grow. If something lands in the PM's queue that the system  should have handled, that is a bug. Fix it. 
Rule 2 — Execution happens first. The PM receives confirmation of what the system did — not a request for  permission to do it. Design every module with this in mind. 
Rule 3 — Defense before offense, always. When regime deteriorates, SLOF comes off before equity trims. PTRH is  never reduced during a downgrade. Survival is the first job.
