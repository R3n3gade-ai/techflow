# ARMS Flight Manual: The Self-Driving Hedge Fund
**A Simple Guide to Our "Brain in a Box"**

### The Big Idea
Imagine you owned a **self-driving car**. You tell it the destination, but the car handles the steering, the brakes, and the speed. It doesn't get tired, it doesn't get scared during a storm, and it follows the rules perfectly every time.

**ARMS** is that car, but for a multi-million dollar investment fund. It’s a "Digital Fortress" that protects the money while the human (the Partner) focuses on the "Big Ideas."

---

### Part 1: The "Brains" (Deciding What to Do)

#### 1. SENTINEL & TDC: The Lie Detectors 🕵️‍♂️
*   **What it is:** A 6-gate security checkpoint.
*   **High School Level:** Before we buy a stock, it has to pass 6 brutal tests. Even after we buy it, a module called **TDC** keeps checking every single day. If the reason we bought the stock is no longer true (like a CEO selling shares or a legal ruling), the system flags it immediately.
*   **The Rule:** "Don't just believe what people say; check the data."

#### 2. MICS: The Smart Better 🎲
*   **What it is:** A formula that decides "Bet Size."
*   **High School Level:** In a normal fund, a human might say, "I really like this stock, let’s put half our money in it!" That’s how people go broke. **MICS** does the math for us. It looks at the test results from SENTINEL and tells us exactly how much to buy. No "gut feelings" allowed.

---

### Part 2: The "Shields" (Staying Safe)

#### 3. ARAS: The Weather Sensor 🌦️
*   **What it is:** A market regime monitor.
*   **High School Level:** Every 5 minutes, the system checks the "market weather." If it senses a storm (a crash) coming, it automatically tells the fund to slow down and sell some risky stuff.

#### 4. PDS: The Emergency Brake 🛑
*   **What it is:** Portfolio Drawdown Sentinel.
*   **High School Level:** This is the "fail-safe." If the fund loses a certain amount of money (like 12%), the system hits the brakes and sells risky positions instantly to protect what's left. It doesn't ask for permission.

#### 5. PTRH: The Insurance Policy 🛡️
*   **What it is:** Tail Risk Hedge.
*   **High School Level:** Just like car insurance, we pay a small fee every month to have "crash protection." If the market drops 20% in one day, our insurance pays out and keeps the fund alive.

---

### Part 3: The "Auto-Pilot" (Making Moves)

#### 6. DSHP: The Cookie Jar Manager 🍪
*   **What it is:** Defensive Sleeve Harvest Protocol.
*   **High School Level:** We keep some money in "Safe Stuff" like Gold. If the price of Gold goes up a lot, we have "extra cookies" in the jar. **DSHP** automatically takes the extra profit and puts it into the bank (cash) so we don't lose it if Gold goes back down.

#### 7. ARES: The Safety Inspector 👷
*   **What it is:** Re-Entry System.
*   **High School Level:** After a big crash, humans usually get too scared to buy back in, or they buy back in too early and get hurt again. **ARES** waits for three specific "Safety Gates" to clear before it starts putting money back into the market in stages (25%, 50%, etc.).

---

### Part 4: Who’s the Boss? (The Tiers)

To make sure things happen fast, the system uses **Tiers**:
*   **Tier 0 (The Robots):** For emergencies (brakes) and math (sizing), the computer acts instantly. 
*   **Tier 1 (The Veto):** For routine trades, the computer says, "I'm doing this in 4 hours." If the human doesn't hit "Stop," it happens automatically.
*   **Tier 2/3 (The Humans):** Only for the biggest, most important decisions that require "Human Wisdom."

### Why This Wins
Most funds fail because **humans are emotional**. They get greedy when things are good and terrified when things are bad. 

**ARMS has no emotions.** It stays disciplined 24/7, follows the math, and keeps a perfect record of every move it makes. It lets the humans be the "Visionaries" while the robots handle the "Execution."

**The Bottom Line:** We built a system that catches what humans miss and executes what humans are too slow to do.
