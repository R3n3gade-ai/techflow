# ARMS Executive Summary: The "Self-Driving" Portfolio
**A Plain-English Guide for Partners**

### The Big Picture
ARMS (Achelion Risk Management System) is like a **self-driving car** for our hedge fund. In a traditional fund, humans do everything: they find ideas, they decide how much to buy, and they try to stay calm when the market crashes. 

**ARMS changes that.** The human (the Partner) picks the destination (the investment ideas), but the computer handles the steering, the speed, and the emergency brakes. This removes human emotion, fatigue, and "gut feelings" from the equation, replacing them with cold, hard math.

---

### 1. How We Pick What to Buy (The "Security Gate")
We don't buy a stock just because we like it. Every idea must pass through a **6-Gate Filter** called **SENTINEL**.
*   **The 6 Tests:** The computer checks if the company is actually important to the future, if it's priced fairly, and if the timing is right. If it fails even one test, we don't buy it.
*   **No More "Gut Feelings":** Once an idea passes the tests, a formula called **MICS** calculates exactly how much to buy. The computer decides the "bet size" based on the data. The human cannot just "double down" because they feel lucky.

### 2. The Three Layers of Protection
We protect the money using three different "safety nets" that work at the same time:

*   **Layer 1: The Weather Sensor (ARAS).** The computer monitors the "market weather" every five minutes. If it senses a "storm" (a crash) coming, it automatically sells some stocks to move us into a safer position.
*   **Layer 2: The Emergency Brake (PDS).** This is our "fail-safe." If the portfolio value drops by a certain percentage (like 12% or 18%), the computer hits the brakes and automatically sells off risky positions to protect what’s left. It doesn't ask for permission; it just does it.
*   **Layer 3: The Insurance Policy (PTRH).** We always carry "insurance" (called QQQ Puts) that pays us if the tech market crashes. Like car insurance, we pay a small fee to make sure we are protected if the worst happens.

### 3. The "Safe" Money (The Defensive Sleeve)
We always keep **14% of our money** in a "Security Vault" (the Defensive Sleeve). This includes things like Gold and Cash. 
*   **The Harvest:** If the price of Gold goes up a lot, the computer automatically sells the "extra" profit and puts it into cash. We call this "harvesting." It ensures our "safe" money stays safe and doesn't become a gamble.

### 4. Who is in Charge? (The Tiers)
To make sure things happen fast, we use **Four Tiers of Control**:
*   **Tier 0 (Computer Only):** For emergencies and basic math, the computer acts instantly. No human can stop it.
*   **Tier 1 (Computer Proposes, Human Can Pause):** For routine trades, the computer says "I'm going to do this in 4 hours." If the human doesn't say "Stop," it happens automatically.
*   **Tier 2 & 3 (Human Required):** This is for big, high-level decisions or brand-new ideas where human wisdom is still better than a machine.

### 5. Why This Wins
Most funds fail because humans get scared when the market goes down or greedy when it goes up. **ARMS doesn't have feelings.** 
*   It stays disciplined 24/7.
*   It trades at the best possible prices to save us money.
*   It keeps a "Black Box" log of every single move, so we can prove exactly why every dollar was spent.

**The Bottom Line:** The computer handles the "how" (the math and the trading), so the Partners can focus on the "what" (the big ideas and the future).
